GDP

Government Expenditure on Education

Top Highest and Lowest PISA Score

Education in each country is highly impacted by the government's policies and also their expenditure. Thus, it is crucial to analyze the government expenditure in proportion of their GDP. Most of the countries spend no more than around 7% of their GDP into their education sector, in which more than 5% is considered as very high. Countries such as Finland, Denmark, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden have consistently spent over 5% of their GDP into their education sector. This aligns with their education quality level that is recognized as some of the best over the world.

On the other hand, developing countries like Armenia, Gambia, Georgia, Madagascar, and Philipines spent around 3-4% of their GDP for their education. However, there does not seem to be a significant correlation between each countries expenditure and their education quality. This is because countries such as Singapore spend less than 5% but they are regarded as a one of the top country with the best education system producing great educated people.

The Program for International Student Assessment (PISA) is an international assessment that measures 15-year-old students' reading, mathematics, and science literacy every 3 years. China reached the champion with the highest PISA score, followed by Singapore and Finland. Meanwhile, the top 8 lowest PISA score are occupied by developing countries.

SCHOOL COMPLETION RATE

Overall, the school completion rate for each degree is pretty high. Most of the continents reached a near complete 100% for each level of education. Africa is the only continent with lower than 80% of completion rate. The variation in education completion rates across different continents is influenced by a complex interplay of historical, socio-economic, cultural, and political factors.

Africa experiences lower education completion rates due to a multitude of challenges. Conflicts, health crises, and political instability also disrupt learning, causing dropouts and incomplete education. Additionally, cultural factors sometimes prioritize early workforce participation over education.

Asia often exhibits relatively good education outcomes, driven by several factors. Governments invest heavily in education infrastructure and teacher training. Additionally, Asia's rapid economic growth has allowed for increased investment in education. Interestingly, Asia has a higher completion rate for secondary level than the primary level.
North and South America can exhibit similar education completion rates when individual countries within these continents prioritize education, invest in infrastructure, and create equitable access to quality education, thereby bridging the gaps that may exist due to regional or continental divisions.

Europe is frequently seen as having some of the best education systems globally. Several factors contribute to this reputation. Europe has mandatory schools, research institutions, comprehensive welfare systems. Thus, Europe completion rate for each education level is always above 90%.

Oceania's lower completion rate for lower secondary compared to higher secondary education is often due to students facing increased academic and logistical challenges in the early stages of education. Higher secondary education may attract more committed students, leading to higher completion rates.

Literacy Worldwide has increase over the years

In 1820, the literacy rate was only as low as 12.05%. The latest data in 2021 shows the literacy worldwide has increased steeply to a percentage of 83.51. Thanks to advancement of technology, compulsory education, and ease to access of education via digital devices! The global trend suggests that literacy will continue to increase, driven by ongoing efforts to improve education, greater access to information through digital technology, and a growing awareness of the importance of literacy for personal and societal development.
